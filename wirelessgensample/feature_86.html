Glossary

Words and ideas used in this book

adaptation: an inherited characteristic that helps a species do something that is important for survival

claim: a statement based on evidence, which often answers a question

compare: to notice how two or more things are alike or different

diagram: a simple illustration that explains how something works or what its parts are

evidence: clues that help explain something or answer a question

explanation: a claim together with the evidence that supports it

extinct: no longer existing on Earth

fossil: evidence of life from the past, such as fossil bones, footprints, or leaf prints

habitat: where a living thing lives and gets everything it needs to survive

identify: to figure out what something is or the group it belongs to

infer: to figure something out based on evidence

inference: something you figure out based on evidence

investigate: to study or try to learn more about something

mammals: a group of animals with hair or fur that give birth to live babies, make milk, and are warm-blooded

observation: information that you gather with your five senses

observe: to use any of the five senses to gather information about something

paleontologist: a scientist who studies species that lived in the past

prey: an animal that is hunted and eaten for food

related: coming from the same family or group of living things

sauropods: a group of dinosaur species that stood on four legs, had long necks and tails, and ate plants

scientific community: scientists around the world who share ways of thinking

species: a group of living things that are more closely related to one another than to any other living things

survive: to stay alive and pass on genes

theropods: a group of dinosaur species that stood on two legs and ate meat
