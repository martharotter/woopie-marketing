The World’s Biggest Dinosaur

The biggest animal ever to walk on land!

I felt like a mouse looking up the giant leg of an elephant.” That’s what Rodolfo Coria remembers about his first look at the huge bones of an unknown dinosaur.

Coria is a paleontologist—a scientist who studies fossils. He lives in Argentina, a country in South America. The bones that made him feel like a mouse were found on his first important dig.

Not all paleontologists study dinosaurs. Many paleontologists study other kinds of extinct animals or plants.

He was helping another paleontologist dig up some unusual bones that had been found in the desert in Argentina. What kind of bones were they? The two paleontologists decided to investigate.

The bones turned out to belong to a kind of dinosaur that no one had known about before. The paleontologists named the dinosaur Argentinosaurus (ar-jen-TEEN-oh-SORE-us) after their country.

Argentinosaurus was bigger than any other dinosaur ever found. In fact, it was the biggest animal ever to walk on land!

Rodolfo Coria soon began leading his own digs to find more bones from Argentinosaurus.
