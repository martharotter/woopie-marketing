Themes and Templates

We focus on the design, you focus on the content

The design and layout of publications are controlled by themes and templates.  We provide templates that work beautifully, along with the tools to customize & add your own layouts and designs. We use form-driven templates which means that it’s simple to add content without worrying about complex layout tools.  

Themes

The visual styling and design of the publication is defined by its theme. The theme sets the tone, or look-and-feel, of the publication. It does this by specifying the typefaces, colors, margins and spacing.

See some of the themes available on our website

These will change and adapt to the browser width, platform and device the publication is being viewed on. For example, on a Kindle 3 the typeface cannot be specified, while on a mobile device a pullquote will be placed inline with the content rather than the side, as there isn’t enough space to move it to the side and still be legible. 

Templates

Individual articles are created from templates. The choice of template is based on the type of content being added - for example a gallery of images would use a different template to a reviews page. The templates currently available are:

Cover

For creating a bold cover page 

Feature

Allows long-form text with media elements such as video and pull-quotes. The most flexible template. Great for long reviews, interviews, opinion pieces, recipes - anything that needs text. 

Divider

A strong image-based article for sectioning content and creating pace and flow within an issue. For example, if a movie magazine had a section on New Cinema Releases and a section on DVD Reviews, a divider would work well between them. 

Gallery

Used for showing a series of large images. 

Listing

These are repeating items of content, where you are entering similar information about several items, for example a book reviews section. We have four types of listings: 

  1. Reviews
  2. Jobs
  3. Events
  4. Groups 

Advertisement

A free-form page for advertising. Currently supports an image with a link, but will allow for user-created HTML and CSS for more powerful, exciting ads.
