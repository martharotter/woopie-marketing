Customizing

Making an article unique

Make each page special (just like this one!)

You can tweak the look of an article if you have some special content requirements. For example, you might have an image gallery of strong black and white images, and wish to put them on a dark background. Or you want to have more colorful page titles, or change around the fonts.

To customize an article, go to the Design tab and change around the values to suite. The colors and fonts are defined on the right of the form, and are based on the theme you have chosen.

You can customize based on your selected theme

What can I change?

  1. Article Background - this is the background color of the entire article.
  2. Heading Background - the background color of the heading block.
  3. Headline - the main title - you can change the color, font used, and style - bold, italic, bold italic , ALL CAPS, or ALL CAPS BOLD
  4. Subhead - the secondary title - you can change the color, font used, and style - bold, italic, bold italic , ALL CAPS, or ALL CAPS BOLD
  5. Credits - the article credits in the heading - you can change the font and color.
  6. Body Text - the text of the article. You can change the font and color.
  7. Link Colors - the color of links. As with the body text, be sure to choose colors that contrast with the article background color.

Once you are finished, click Save Draft and select the Preview tab to see how it looks.

Power Mode

If you're comfortable with CSS, you can add your own CSS in the "Add Custom CSS" textbox - this will apply your custom CSS to this page. Custom CSS gives you a lot more control over the individual page's look-and-feel.
