Exporting Your Publication

Package up your publication for many devices

The final stage in outputting your publication is exporting it. This will then create a number of different assets for you:

  • Web We output a complete bundle for use on the web. This includes all the image and media files you have uploaded, along with the required javascript, stylesheet and web pages you need. This version is responsive, so it will resize for mobile, tablet and desktop. You can FTP this version to your own website, place it behind a paywall, and/or add your own code if you so wish.
  • ePub We also give you an ePub file. This is perfect for modern ereaders such as iBooks on the iPhone and iPad or the Nook.
  • Mobi This is another eReader format, which works best on Amazon Kindle.

Download your files

The easiest way to export your content is simply by downloading it to your desktop. From there you can submit them to Amazon or iBooks, upload to your own webserver, or put them behind a paywall.

Generating Publication

Publications are exported from the Article Listing screen.

Generate formats

Generate the different packages by checking the boxes of the formats you require, such as Web, Epub, Mobi, and iOS. This will begin the process of packaging your articles and assets into the various formats.

Now you're ready to export your publication.

Exporting your Publication

Once Woopie has finished generating your output, you can click item 3: "Publish and Export."

Download formats

The download process may take several minutes depending on the number of articles and the amount of assets you've added to your publication. Once downloading is complete, you'll have all the files you need!

Additional Publishing Options

Dropbox: Dropbox is a great way to enable testing of your publication on other devices. You can connect your Dropbox account from the Manage Account tab, and when you do, the "Export to Dropbox" option will be enabled for you.

Newsstand: If you're using Woopie's iOS Newsstand application, the "Send to Newsstand" option will be enabled for you to push your issue live to iOS Newsstand subscribers.

FTP: Lastly, if your content gets pushed to an external server which we've connected for you, you'll see the "FTP" option enabled so you can both push content live as well as update instantly whenever you're ready.
