Le Cool Guides

Made in Italy

When you think ‘quality craftsmanship’, you automatically think ‘Italy’. It is only right, then, that Italy’s fashion folk go to incredible lengths to ensure that their garments are as authentically Italian as possible. It’s this integrity and loyalty to the artisan traditions of this nation, steeped in history, that makes goods with the ‘Made in Italy’ label so covetable.

While we might buy Made in Italy on a weekly basis (Parmigiano, anyone?), we also gravitate towards Made in Italy for beautiful garments, because of their innate style, flattering cuts, and for the sheer want of a little luxury. Borsalino is the holy grail of millinery for all hat fanatics. Their unmistakable felt hat takes about 7 weeks to make, and Borsalino have been making them since 1857 - the political uproar of the Risorgimento crucially inspired their design. Since then, they’ve been in nearly every movie worth seeing - yup, Bogart wears one in Casablanca.

A less established, but just as exciting young upstart in the Made in Italy world is Coliac by Martina Graselli, whose jewellery collections are full of geometric and abstract wonder. Her A/W13 collection ‘Zang Tumb Tumb’ is a myriad of rustic stones set against glistening metallic backdrops, ethnic and tribal, but not contrived. Expect to see a lot more of her in the fashion glossies very soon...
