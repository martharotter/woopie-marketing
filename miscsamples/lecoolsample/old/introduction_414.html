Le Cool Guides

A Note From le cool

Ireland and Italy have more than the letter ‘I’, tricolors and a love of tweed sports jackets in common; we have the same work ethic. When we know we’re doing something right, we feel duty-bound to export it on an international scale.

In this second booklet exploring the new icons of Italian design, we see Paola Pivi ripping up the New York art world with her bold installations, while photographer Francesco Carrozzini is directly responsible for some of the hottest music videos you’re likely to see come out of the US this year. CO|TE, the fresh young Made In Italy fashion label has style mavens all over the world tripping over their heels to get a slice of Italian fashion flair, while graphic designers like KMZero, Francesco Franchi quietly work away, pushing the boundaries of design. In the same vein, furniture designers such as Flou are pushing back the Scandinavian tide with pure, unadulterated innovation and bravery. Furniture that lights up? Well done, sir.

While we were having our breath taken away by the impossibly fabulous Italian design we discovered while researching this booklet, we reminded ourselves that we too are dancing on the international design stage. Irish design shone at the London Design Festival, Wallpaper magazine is all over the Irish Crafts Council, and Irish fashion is having a moment in the sun with fashion designers Simone Rocha and Danielle Romeril leading the pack.

We join Peroni Nastro Azzurro in clinking our glasses and toasting the authenticity, innate style and unique features that set Italian design apart whilst keeping an eye on the next chapter which will be written at home.
