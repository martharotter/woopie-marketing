Seasons and Weather

Question 1 of 5: Which factors cause Earth’s seasons?

  • spring and autumn equinoxes
  • earth’s tilt and revolution around the sun
  • air movement and water vapor
  • convectional, orographic, and frontal precipitation
Check Answer

As the earth revolves around the sun, it is tilted at a 23.5° angle in relation to the sun. Because of the earth’s revolution and its tilt, different parts of the earth receive the direct rays of the sun for more hours of the day at certain times in the year.

Question 2 of 5: What causes summer in the northern hemisphere?

  • The northern half of Earth tilts away from the sun.
  • The northern half of Earth tilts toward the sun.
  • The southern half of Earth tilts upward.
  • The southern half of Earth tilts downward.
Check Answer

The northern half of the earth tilts toward the sun in summer and away from the sun in winter.

Question 3 of 5: What is the difference between weather and climate?

  • Weather refers to the effects of convectional precipitation; climate refers to frontal precipitation.
  • Weather conditions are caused by earth’s tilt; climatic conditions are caused by earth’s rotation.
  • Weather refers to the condition of the atmosphere at one time; climate refers to weather conditions over a long time.
  • Weather consists of long-term patterns of air movement; climate involves daily shifts in air movement.
Check Answer

Weather is the condition of the atmosphere at a particular location and time. Climate is the term for weather conditions at a particular location over a long period of time.

Question 4 of 5: Which correctly describes a rain shadow and identifies the type of precipitation it is associated with?

  • an area of little precipitation on the windward side of a mountain; vaporous precipitation
  • a heavy rain that often leads to severe flooding or mudslides; convectional precipitation
  • a stationary front of precipitation that falls throughout the winter; frontal precipitation
  • an area of little precipitation on the leeward side of a mountain; orographic precipitation
Check Answer

The land on the leeward side is called a rain shadow because it gets little rain from the descending dry air.

Question 5 of 5: Which type of extreme weather involves powerful storms that form over warm, tropical ocean waters?

  • hurricanes
  • tornados
  • blizzards
  • drought
Check Answer

Storms that form over warm, tropical ocean waters are called hurricanes—also known as typhoons in Asia.
