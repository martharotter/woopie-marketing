Foreword

by Cameron Koczon

Chris Shiflett and I started Brooklyn Beta under the mantra of, ‘make something you love.’ Take a look, dearest of dear readers, at what you hold in your hands (this may require grasping your monitor, or picking up your tablet or smartphone). That warmth radiating through your palms (or aforementioned device) is love. And love is what we need more of in this weird, 2D industry of ours. We need more ways of adding depth — a little meat and bones — to our online relationships.

We’ve been trying. We devised 48 x 48 representations of ourselves to compensate for not being able to see each other face-to-face. We tap out 140-character quips and blog posts (R.I.P.?) because we don’t have traditional conversations. We take filtered, square photos of every latte, sunset, or adorable baby or kitten moment, so we can share experiences from afar.

This is a very now-centric kind of storytelling and we’re getting good at it. I can easily find out what you’re thinking, reading, watching, and feeling right now. What I can’t get is context. I have little sense of the past that brought you to that thought, or the future that will bring you to your next thought or latte photo.

We need more of this kind of context. We need ‘insite’ into one another’s past and future if we’re going to grow closer as a community. And it’s only through such closeness that we’ll have a real shot at driving the web and the world in a meaningful direction. The interviews in this book will help us. After reading through them, Tina, Tim, Andy, Cameron, Jason, Jeffrey, Sarah, Trent, Simon, Jon, Ron, Josh, Ethan, Alex, Brendan, Mandy, Jim, Dan, Ian, Kevin, and Daniel feel more real to me. Even the folks who I see on the regular have become more vivid. We owe Keir and Elliot a hearty thanks for putting so much love into this work. More please.
