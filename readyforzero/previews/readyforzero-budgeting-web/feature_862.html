Chapter Two: Building a Savings

Alright, we’ve got budgeting covered. But we don’t just budget to spend less on certain things or free up room for debt payoff and other goals. We also budget so that we can save for the future. And that in and of itself can be tricky.

If this is the first time you’ve ever really thought about putting money in a savings account, don’t worry. This section will include everything you need to know about creating and growing a savings fund that will keep your financial future bright.

Introduction to Savings

If you’ve ever wondered how you should save, what you should save for, or what the point is of saving money in an account that earns little interest, then you’ve come to the right place. While the ideal amount of savings differs for everyone, it's very important to have some money set aside for emergencies. Take a look at these articles to learn about how to choose a savings account and how to start building an emergency fund.

  • Types of Savings Accounts and How to Choose the Best One (Careful Cents)
  • Building an Emergency Fund (PT Money)

Savings Strategies

If saving money were easy to do, we’d all be buffing up our savings accounts every month. But between rising costs of living and salaries that don’t always catch up, it can be hard to make ends meet from month to month, much less put money aside. Luckily, there are plenty of personal finance gurus who talk about how to save on even the tightest of budgets. Here are a few:

  • 9 Ways to Make Saving Automatic (Brip Blap)
  • Methods to Budget and Save Money #1: The Envelope System (Broke Professionals)

Teaching the Next Generation to Save

What’s the best way to make sure that the financial problems of our day don’t extend on into the future? Teach the next generation to save! Yes, most kids may prefer playing with their friends over talking about money with you – but if you make it fun for them then you can start incorporating lessons early on. Not convinced? Read some of these articles for help in getting started:

  • How to Talk to Kids About Money (GoGirl Finance)
  • Good Money Habits for Children – It’s Never Too Early to Start (Cash Money Life)
