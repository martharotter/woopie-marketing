Day 2: Mindfulness

If you’re feeling intimidated by the scope of the challenge, you’re not alone. You’ve already taken the first step to making a lifestyle change by making your pledge, but finding the motivation to follow through is often the hardest part. By remembering to break the challenge into manageable steps you can remind yourself that no goal is accomplished overnight. Working step-by-step is the surest way to make steady progress. And what’s the most important first step? Mindfulness.

Mindfulness is being aware of the impact your decisions have on your present and future well-being and intentionally choosing more positive (although sometimes difficult) decisions. It’s the difference between making decisions on auto-pilot and sticking to a behavior out of habit versus actually stopping to recognize the choices you’re making. If you’re not truly aware of the decisions you make, you can’t distinguish what decisions need to change.

Mindfulness provides clarity and empowers you to turn habitual behaviors that are causing you problems into new, more beneficial behaviors. Being mindful of your actions is one small step that will ignite the process of change, leading you steadily to your goals.

Food fuels your body and provides essential nutrients that you need to stay at peak health and accomplish all the goals you have for yourself. It’s not just an item to mark off your list but rather something that serves an essential function in your life. Food is meant to be enjoyed and its ultimate purpose is to nourish.

If you focus on food as simply a “need-to-do,” you’ll likely choose convenience over health. That’s where mindfulness comes in. By being aware of your food choices and the impact they have on your physical well-being, you’re more likely to prioritize healthy eating - even at the cost of convenience or desire. Suddenly you may find that carving out the time to cook or carving out room in your budget for healthier food becomes more desirable than taking the easy way out with a quick meal that isn’t as good for you.

Kath from Kath Eats is a food blogger who shared some advice on how making mindful (and healthy) additions to your life can help you change your habits:

“It's easier to add than subtract, so start with some healthy additions. When you focus on adding healthy habits you'll create a snowball effect of motivation that will then lead you to subtract your unhealthier habits. Add a salad, a workout, or an hour of sleep before you attempt to subtract your grande mocha, a TV session on the couch, or your nightly cocktail. Once you develop your new healthy additions it will be easier to remove the unhealthy ones.” – Kath, Kath Eats

Your body goes through a lot on a day to day basis! But to keep it healthy and strong requires a certain amount of maintenance. If you truly appreciate all that your body does for you - and what it needs from you in return - you may find it more of a pressing need to keep it strong and healthy.

This understanding of your body’s needs is the first step to practicing mindfulness in your fitness regime. Working out isn’t always fun or easy, but it is the only way to keep your body fit and strong. If skipping a workout is more appealing to you now but you are aware that it will prevent you from maintaining and building strength, then your decision will be more likely to prioritize your long-term needs. Like everything else, this isn’t a decision you make once and for all. It’s a choice you make each day - and each day you can start anew.

ReadyForZero Community Manager Shannon McNay talks about how mindfulness in fitness can make a difference:

“A few years ago, I had as solid of a fitness regime as you could imagine...and then I started working for a startup. Suddenly the nights when I came home and sat to decompress from the long hours far outnumbered the nights I came home and worked out.

I knew that my health could suffer from this, if not now then in the years to come. But I was always so tired when I got home that I didn’t actively think about it. I succumbed to my lack of energy and like a zombie watched tv or read until bedtime, not giving a single thought to my health.

There was no light bulb moment for me. The desire to get back to my old workout routine ate at me until I finally took back the control of my life. Now I force myself to think about what being sedentary after work really does to me before I sit down. Maybe somedays I’m still too tired and have to take it easy. On those days I still choose to workout, but I do a light relaxation yoga class (from an app on my phone) instead of a more intense workout. The point is, I’m not on autopilot anymore. One way or the other I do something.” - Shannon McNay, ReadyForZero

Every time we swipe our card or hand over cash we’re making a financial choice. Because we make financial transactions regularly, it’s easy to forget the real impact these everyday transactions have on our larger financial goals.

You can remain in control of your finances by being mindful of each financial decision you make and how it will affect your overall finances. This will also help you to stay within your monthly budget and stay focused on your long term goals.

Glen Craig from Free From Broke shares some wisdom on the importance of mindfulness in your financial decisions:

“Mindfulness in your finances is so important. For me that's what began my adventure to get out of debt and build up my finances. I didn't pay close attention to where I was spending and it cost me. When you start to watch where your money is going you get a bigger picture of more than just your money -- you get a picture of your lifestyle.

See, every time you spend a dollar you're spending a bit of your hard work. You put time and energy into that dollar and you need to respect that. When you're mindful and conscious of your spending you question whether a purchase is worth the hard work you put into the money. The things you buys aren't just "things" they are validations of your lifestyle. This way of thinking is powerful and can transform your goals. When you spend you ask yourself "is this adding to my goals or taking away from them?"

This takes practice but anyone can do it. Get yourself into the habit of questioning the purchases you make. If what you're about to buy isn't worth the effort you put into the money and it isn't working toward your goals then you know to move on. Give it a try and see how it can transform the way you look at your financial choices.” – Glen Craig, Free From Broke

Remember that one good thing leads to another and that requires first starting with that “one good thing.” You don’t have to start out with a bang. The most important thing is that you start. Every mindful decision you make along the way will convert to a single positive change. You’re on your way.

  • Practice mindfulness in each decision you make every day
  • Recognize the long term impact each decision will have on your goals
  • Break out of habits and build awareness
