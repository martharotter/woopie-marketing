Chapter Four: Planning a Future Together

By now you and your partner should practically be experts at planning and strategizing together. Now it's time to use those newfound skills on the fun part: planning a future together.

More Self-Reflection

Just like you did with budgeting, take some time to determine your individual goals first. What do you want in the next year, five years, 30 years? Don’t think about what you should want or what you think your partner wants. Think about what you want. There will be plenty of time for you two to come together after this.

These goals don’t just revolve around your finances. Think about your career, where you want to live, what dreams you would like to see fulfilled, and more. Financial freedom gives you the freedom to explore things that you may not have considered before. Figuring out what you want when you have that freedom is a great way to give yourself a prize to set your sights on.

Come Together to Set Priorities

Sound familiar? This is a similar step to that mentioned earlier in the book - but this time we're talking about non-financial priorities and goals. Since you’ve both taken time to look inward and discover your true desires, you’ll be ready to talk about how you can accomplish them together.

Be open and honest about what you want during this talk. You could find out things about your partner - or yourself - that surprise you. But that’s the beauty of it! Maybe you’ve secretly been dreaming of embarking on a freelance career while your partner has secretly been dreaming of traveling the world. While those are two different goals, they can work in action together. And working to achieve financial freedom together will bring you closer to turning those dreams into reality.

It can’t be emphasized enough how important it is to focus on what you want, not on what you or your partner feel is expected of you. We no longer live in a world where people get one job and stay there for 40 years. While good and bad, the changes are here now which means your own future has changed possibilities too. Don’t limit yourself to anything you think is expected of you or is what you’re “supposed” to do. Dream big and you’ll never know what you’ll accomplish!

Once you’ve decided on your goals, figure out how you can work to achieve them side by side. You may find that something needs to get tabled for awhile if your partner’s goals are more time sensitive. Or you may find that you can do both at the same time, but maybe scaled back. Then there’s the wonderful possibility that your goals work perfectly together. No matter the case, figure out what your short and long term priorities are so you can move on to making them happen.

Create Tangible Methods to Achieve

Just like everything else, plot out a strategy to achieve these newfound priorities and help each other stay motivated. Then remind each other of what you’ve set your sights on so you can stick together when times get tough. It can be as simple as putting a picture representing the goal (or goals) on your refrigerator, sending your partner encouraging messages throughout the day, or plotting the milestone dates on your calendar. Keep your goals in sight at all times so you can be sure you’re on the journey together.
