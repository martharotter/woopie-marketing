Working Together

A grassroots effort driven by people in each community

Make-A-Wish has been a different sort of charity from Day One … a grassroots effort driven by people in each community who recognize that a wish experience can make life better for kids with life-threatening medical conditions.

Our chapters give life to our mission. And Make-A-Wish America provides services, resources and best practices that help them grant wishes with the potential to change lives. The Chapter Support team is at the forefront of helping Make-A-Wish staff members nationwide.

Wish Fulfillment Fund

Make-A-Wish chapters have one priority – do everything possible to grant wishes in the communities it serves.

Sometimes, chapters will have funds to grant more wishes, but not enough people or outreach. That’s where the Make-A-Wish America Wish Fulfillment Fund steps in. It allows funds raised in one territory to help another chapter that faces fundraising challenges. It honors the donor’s intent while still acknowledging that wish granting is about every eligible child in every community.

Chief Medical Advisor

Medical professionals have made incredible strides in giving patients a fighting chance against their conditions. Make-A-Wish needs to maintain pace with these advances, to understand what conditions should qualify children for wishes. And we must connect with the medical community to refer children.

Dr. Jonathan Stein, accomplished pediatrician and long-time advisor for Make-A-Wish Connecticut, joined us in FY12 as our chief medical advisor. Dr. Stein’s expert guidance will help us ensure that our donated resources go to medically eligible children.

People know why it is important to help Make-A-Wish kids through unimaginable challenges.

They recognize the essential goodness of our mission. And for that, we honor them through our giving societies. Each society recognizes people and foundations based on their level and method of giving. Together, they personify unequaled generosity and care.
