Judgement is Coming

What the Drokk? A decent Dredd movie?

Director - Pete Travis
Cast - Karl Urban, Olivia Thirlby, Lena Headey
Certificate - 18
Running time - 95 minutes

Let's get this out of the way - this Dredd movie completely blows the 1995 'Judge Dredd' crapfest out of the water. Karl Urban's Judge Dredd is the Dredd I grew up with as a kid reading 2000AD every week. He's tough, laconic, unflappable - just one tough son-of-a-bitch. If you loved the comic book Dredd, you will really enjoy this movie.

Set in a dystopian future where nuclear war has turned most of America into an irradiated wasteland called the Cursed Earth, mankind finds itself packed into sprawling mega-cities. On the East coast, Mega City One is a vast metropolis of over 800 million citizens, most of whom are unemployed. To combat this powder keg of violence due process has been dispensed with and a new breed of law enforcement has arisen - the Judges. They have the power of Judge, Jury and Executioner, able to administer instant judgement on the streets.

Dredd follows the titular protagonist on an assessment patrol with rookie Judge Anderson. Anderson is a borderline failing cadet with a unique quality - fallout from the nuclear devastation has given her a helpful genetic mutation - powerful psychic mind-reading capabilities. Crime is spiralling as a new drug called "Slo-mo" that slows time for the user is plaguing the city - an experience that lends itself to some beautiful visuals of everything from iridescent bubbles and shattering glass to horrifying slow-motion injuries as bullets shred cheeks and guts.

The two Judges respond to a routine call to Peach Trees, a massive 200 story slum controlled by Ma-Ma (played by Lena Headey) and her gang of vicious thugs. When the Judges capture an important member of Ma-Ma's gang, they become trapped in the building and must fight their way out. That's basically the whole movie - lots and lots of shooting and bullets flying and people getting killed. There's also the world's most stupidest location for a skating park, but that's for another day.

Comic fans should keep an eye out (no pun intended - you'll see why) for some classic graffiti as well - 'Chopper', 'Muties Out' and a 'Kenny Who?' are some of the ones I spotted.

Plot-wise, it's very similar to the film The Raid (which is excellent) but with way, way more guns. Dredd's Lawgiver weapon is a highlight, with its voice-controlled selection of special bullets from High Explosive and Incendiary to the head-melting Hotshot. Karl Urban's performance as Dredd is note-perfect - there's no arc here, no character development. Dredd is Dredd, a no-nonsense officer of the law who will carry out his duty without fear or question. Comic fans should keep an eye out (no pun intended - you'll see why) for some classic graffiti as well - 'Chopper', 'Muties Out' and a 'Kenny Who?' are some of the ones I spotted.

The best scene of the film for me was [SPOILER] where Dredd tosses one of the gang members over the balcony, then turns his back without saying a word. In any other film, the lead would have looked the antagonist in the eye after and come out with a witty one-liner ("Just dropping off the trash") but Dredd just does it and turns around without missing a beat. He's not making a point or settling a score - he's just doing his job and moving onto the next piece of scum. The guy wasn't special, just another perp.

End of Spoiler

Much of the action takes place inside a crumbling apartment complex so there's not a lot of sprawling sci-fi vistas. In the comics Mega City One is a forest of bizarrely shaped towering blocks, twisting on-ramps and hover trucks , as if the Sydney Opera House was mutated with a fungal virus. The Mega City One of the movie (which was based on Johannesburg) is much more 21st century - a sprawling grid of streets and slums peppered with functional skyscrapers. The vehicles (and this was one of creator/producer John Wagner's few criticisms) are what you'd see now, rather than the cool souped up concept vehicles from films like 'I, Robot' and 'Total Recall' (reviewed this issue)

Minor niggles aside, overall it's a fantastic movie that uses its 3D format effectively rather than gratuitously. For Dredd lovers and fans of films like Robocop and The Dark Knight, it's a must-see. No origin story, no padding, just intense, violent, judgment.
