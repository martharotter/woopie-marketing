The Hunger Games

*****

Director - Gary Ross
Cast - Jennifer Lawrence, Josh Hutcherson and Liam Hemsworth
Certificate - PG13
Running time - 142 minutes

If you see only one film about teenage runaways being chased by children through the woods involving bows and arrows, then rent out 'Moonlight Kingdom' - if you see two, then you could do worse than 'The Hunger Games'. Set in a dystopian future, the nation of Panem (in a subtle nod to the Latin phrase for 'Bread and Circuses') holds a regular 'Battle Royale' between the Twelve Districts. A boy and a girl from each district are selected and must fight to the death, until only one survives. The 'tributes' are selected either by lottery or by volunteering, and placed in a controlled environment where they must hunt and kill each other.

Based on the popular book by Suzanne Collins (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suzanne_Collins) the movie is a reasonably entertaining adventure story, though it might be a bit dark for its intended tweenage 'Twlight' fan audience. The book is a lot more gory than the film (for some reason children getting impaled is fine to write about but not to show) but it's still pretty violent. Readers of the book will notice a few things missing and a few different decisions, but overall it's a fairly faithful adaptation.

Jennifer Lawrence makes for a great lead as Katniss, though her delivery can be a bit flat and repetitive. (Though that may be because she spends a lot of the movie waking up in trees.) Josh Hutcherson's Peeta falls flat as he just isn't that likeable a character (plus how does a district in abject poverty produce a baker with outstanding icing skills?). Woody Harrelson makes for a great minor character as the drunk Haymitch Abernathy (fun fact: as Woody is a strict vegetarian, his character is never seen eating meat.) The sets and effects are all very exciting and visual - if you love bold sci-fi costumes then you are in for a treat. Overall, a solid rent-it-on-DVD type film.

Lockout

*****

Director - James Mather
Cast - Guy Pearce, Maggie Grace and Peter Stormare
Certificate - PG13
Running time - 95 minutes

Take the 'sarcastic, one-liner quipping badass' from 'Escape from New York' (oh, and bring the plot about rescuing the President, because we're going to need something very similar). Throw in the high-tech prison scenario from 'Fortress' and that's your movie.

Guy Pearce must have wanted a new house or something because this isn't really the high-brow, intense role you normally see him in. He plays Snow, an ex-con or FBI guy or something who must rescue Liam Neeson's daughter from a space prison after she is Taken by the inmates when they all escape after plot happens. He plays it for laughs, coming out exclusively in badass sarcastic replies to pretty much every situation. There's lots of shooting and a crazy turn by Joseph Gilgun (This is England) as a psychotic Scotsman.

If you think that all sounds a bit silly, you should see the science. There's a bit where they jump out of a space-station and plummet down to Earth. I don't think they understood the gravity of the situation. It's basically nonsense but if you've seen everything in the video shop you could do worse.

Batman!

*****

Director - Christopher Nolan
Cast - Christian Bale, Tom Hardy and Anne Hathaway
Certificate - PG13
Running time - 165 minutes

Batman! Batman! It's BATMAN!! There's not much more to say about this movie. If you liked the first too (and if you didn't you probably don't like movies much and should go read something else) then you'll enjoy this. It's got all the stuff you liked from the other Batmans (Batmen?) â€¦ BATMAN! Alfred! (who's buried enough members of the Wayne fabilee) Gadgets! Training montage! It's also really really long so bring your Batather if you have one.

'The Dark Knight Rises' also manages to avoid the classic Third Superhero Movie mistake of having Too Many Villains. 'Spiderman 3' did this with Sandman and Venom and Green Goblin knocking around. 'Batman Forever' (the one with Val Kilmer) did this with The Riddler and Two Face. Here (and no spoilers here) the main focus is on Bane, a brutal terrorist, strategist and tough guy. Ludicrously voiced by Tom Hardy, he's one tough SOB.

Influenced by the comics 'The Dark Knight Returns' (an aged Batman makes a comeback - go buy it today), 'Knightfall' (Bane beats Batman physically and mentally) and 'No Man's Land' (Gotham becomes a cut-off war zone), there is a lot going on in this movie, and some strange time-line issues, but overall it's a fantastic ending to a great, dark, intelligent superhero trilogy.

Yup, 'Total Recall' is not great, and not really worth the remake. The Arnie one was dumb but fun, while this one is a bit serious and meh. Colin Farrell plays Douglas Quad, a man who goes for a 'memory vacation' and accidentally unlocks the fact that he might be a spy (or is it all a dream?) and ends up on the run from the director's wife Kate Beckinsale, who's great at this punchy-running-fighting stuff. He runs away from her. That's the movie.

There's also a giant tunnel through the Earth that makes no sense (especially as they seem to have gotten the flipping-upside down thing the wrong way around) and Walter from Breaking Bad who's a bit wasted here. The action is good, the CGI and effects and all that are great (if you like movies with futuristic UIs like 'Minority Report' and 'Iron Man' you'll get a kick out of this) but the film lacks a heart.
