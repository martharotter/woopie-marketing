More about Meg Gardiner

Who is Meg Gardiner?

Meg is an American now living in Austin, Texas, having spent many years in the UK. The daughter of an English professor, she went to law school, practiced law then taught law in what could be called her other life. Now we know her as a crime-fiction creator and author of the Evan Delaney and Jo Beckett series, as well as a number of standalone novels.

Meg is no stranger to awards. Her first novel, China Lake, received the 2009 Edgar Award for Best Paperback Original novel when it was published in the United States in 2008. Her first Jo Beckett novel, The Dirty Secrets Club, won the 2009 The Romantic Times Reviewers’ Choice award for Best Procedural Novel and her ninth novel, The Nightmare Thief, won the 2012 Audie Award for Thriller/Suspense Audiobook of the Year.

Who are her characters?

Evan’s a freelance journalist from California. She’s spirited, quick-witted and fights hard for the people she loves. She may be too brash for her own good, which is why she’s on her second set of teeth, but she has a warm heart. She thinks the world is tragic and so you’d better laugh whenever you can. Just don’t get on her bad side, because she may have a heat-seeking missile stashed in her car.

Jo is law enforcement’s last resort in baffling cases, but she’s not a cop. She’s a doctor — a forensic psychiatrist who analyzes dead people for the police. She’s a deadshrinker. Jo doesn’t pick up gory bits of trace evidence with tweezers. She digs into people’s passions, obsessions and secrets to find out what killed them. Her territory is the psyche and the human heart.
