Message from the Chief Executive Officer


10

In Sierra Leone’s capital Freetown, we managed 10 burial teams and two cemeteries to ensure not just a safe but a dignified burial for ebola victims.


94,334

vulnerable children benefited from our education work in Kenya – more than three times our target. We worked with 2,796 teachers, 1,344 members of school management boards and 226 head teachers.




77

We supported healthcare in 77 health facilities and six refugee settlements in Uganda.

CALLED ‘THE WORST CRISIS PEOPLE HAVE NEVER HEARD OF ’ BY US AMBASSADOR SAMANTHA POWER, THE SEVERITY OF THE SITUATION IN CENTRAL AFRICAN REPUBLIC LED THE UNITED NATIONS (UN) TO DECLARE IT A TOP PRIORITY OR LEVEL THREE EMERGENCY – A CATEGORY RESERVED FOR THE MOST SEVERE , LARGE-SCALE AND COMPLEX CRISES THAT INCLUDES SYRIA , THE PHILIPPINES AND SOUTH SUDAN.

By April 2014, despite the security and funding challenges in Central African Rebublic, one of the world’s poorest countries, Concern had a team on the ground responding to the immediate needs of the men, woman and children who were seriously struggling to survive. It’s what we do.
